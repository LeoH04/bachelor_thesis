Drawbacks noted: difficulty communicating ideas, egocentric attitude, limited willingness for further education. Alternatives considered: Candidate A (strong technical and leadership but unfriendly, unorganized, poor at criticism), Candidate B (high conscientiousness and stress handling but pretentious, grumpy, uncooperative), Candidate D (excellent problem‑solving and responsibility but arrogant, moody, weak leadership). Main trade‑off: Candidate C’s cooperation positives outweigh his communication and attitude drawbacks better than the more severe uncooperative or arrogance seen in other candidates. Remaining uncertainty: extent to which communication difficulty and egocentric behavior will affect day‑to‑day crew interaction on long‑distance flights. What would change my vote: convincing evidence that Candidate B’s uncooperative behavior is occasional and mitigated, or that Candidate A’s unfriendly/unorganized traits are minor and do not impact safety, or that Candidate D’s arrogance does not affect crew dynamics.
- Evidence That Could Change My Mind
Strong evidence that the cooperation issues of other candidates are minor or manageable, and that Candidate C’s communication and egocentric traits are less impactful than perceived.
