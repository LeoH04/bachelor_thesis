- Candidate B: concrete examples of teamwork or cooperative behavior.
- Candidate A: evidence of reliability and teamwork.
- Candidate C: willingness to pursue further professional development and how egocentric behavior affects crew dynamics.

What would change the decision
- Positive, specific evidence that Candidate D can remain calm and work well with crew could elevate D as a viable option.
- Demonstrated cooperative behavior from Candidate B could mitigate concerns and make B a stronger contender.
- Evidence that Candidate C improves communication and commits to further training would solidify C’s lead.
- Proof that Candidate A can reliably work in a team and address health habits could raise A’s suitability.

What to ask or look for next
- Ask any agent for observed instances of Candidate D handling an emergency or high‑stress scenario.
- Request examples or testimonies of Candidate B’s interaction with crew members.
- Inquire whether Candidate C has plans for additional certifications or training.
- Seek any information on Candidate A’s reliability record or teamwork experiences.
