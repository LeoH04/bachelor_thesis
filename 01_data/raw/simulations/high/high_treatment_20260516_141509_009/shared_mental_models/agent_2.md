## Task Summary
- Goal: Choose the best candidate for a long-distance pilot position at an airline.
- Candidates: Candidate A, Candidate B, Candidate C, Candidate D

## Candidate Evaluation
| Candidate | Evidence For | Evidence Against | Fit for Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Candidate A | Anticipates dangerous situations; sees complex connections; excellent spatial vision; very good leadership qualities | Regarded as a show‑off; not open to new ideas; no data on stress handling or teamwork/communication | Moderate – strong situational awareness and leadership but unknown stress tolerance and crew interaction; cultural fit concerns | |
| Candidate B | Very conscientious; handles stress very well; good at assessing weather conditions; excellent computer skills | Weak memory for numbers; makes nasty remarks about colleagues; no explicit data on teamwork/leadership | Moderate – good stress handling and technical skills, but interpersonal issues and memory weakness could affect safety | |
| Candidate C | Makes correct decisions quickly; keeps calm in a crisis; understands complicated technology; 100 % reliable; creates a positive atmosphere with the crew | Difficulty communicating ideas; regarded as egocentric; not very willing to further his education | Strong – reliability, calmness, technical competence and positive crew influence outweigh communication and development gaps for long‑distance ops | |
| Candidate D | Responds to unexpected events adequately; concentrates very well; solves problems extremely well; takes responsibility seriously | Regarded as a know‑it‑all; hot temper; relatively weak leadership skills; no data on teamwork/communication or stress handling | Weak – problem‑solving and responsibility are good, but temperament and lack of evidence on stress/crew interaction are safety‑relevant concerns | |

## My Position
- My Last Vote: Candidate C
- My Current Working Favorite: Candidate C
- My Rationale: Candidate C combines reliability, calm judgment, solid technical competence, and a generally positive crew atmosphere. While communication difficulties and limited appetite for further education are drawbacks, they are less immediately safety‑critical than the memory weakness of Candidate B or the temperament issues of Candidate D. Candidate A lacks any data on stress handling and shows cultural‑fit concerns. Therefore, based on the available evidence, Candidate C is the strongest overall fit for a long‑distance pilot role.

## Other Agents' Positions

|---|---|---|---|---|

| agent_3 | Candidate C | Candidate C | Considers C’s reliability, calmness, technical competence, and positive crew atmosphere outweigh communication and development gaps; sees other candidates’ deficits as more safety‑critical. | Moderate |

## Emerging Group View
- Group‑Leading Candidate: Candidate C
- Important Agreements: Agents 1, 2, and 3 all favor Candidate C, agreeing that its strengths outweigh the weaknesses.
- Important Disagreements / Tensions: None identified.
- Uncertainties: Still lack explicit data on stress/pressure performance for Candidates A and D, teamwork/communication for Candidates D and B, and on Candidate C’s performance under prolonged high‑stress, long‑duration flight conditions and deeper technical competence.

## Open Questions and Next‑Step Focus
- Missing evidence remains:
  - How does Candidate A handle stress and perform under high‑pressure, long‑duration flight conditions?
  - How does Candidate D handle stress/pressure on long flights?
  - What are Candidate D’s teamwork and communication skills with crew?
  - What are Candidate B’s teamwork/crew‑communication abilities and leadership style?
  - How does Candidate C perform under high‑stress, long‑duration flight conditions, and what is his technical competence depth (e.g., navigation, systems)?
- What would change the decision:
  - Positive data showing strong stress tolerance and effective crew communication for A and D would raise them as top contenders.
  - Evidence that B’s interpersonal behavior does not impair crew coordination would improve his standing.
  - Demonstrated willingness of C to pursue further education and improved communication would mitigate his weaknesses.
- What to ask or look for next:
  - Since agents have confirmed no internal data, the group may need to seek external sources (e.g., simulator performance records, interview feedback) to fill the gaps.
