## My Position
<!-- SMM_SECTION:my_position:start -->
After reviewing all available attributes and the remaining gaps (no explicit reliability or responsibility data for Candidate A; no additional strengths for Candidate D beyond the four listed), I have weighed each candidate against the key criteria for a long‑distance pilot:

- **Candidate A** – Strong situational awareness (anticipates danger, sees complex connections, excellent spatial vision) and good leadership, but the lack of reliability/responsibility evidence and noted weaknesses (show‑off attitude, resistance to new ideas, unorganized, poor at taking criticism) raise safety concerns for long‑haul planning and crew interaction.
- **Candidate B** – Very conscientious, handles stress well, excels at weather assessment and computer‑based tasks, and demonstrates strong technical and cognitive competence—critical for long‑distance operations. Interpersonal drawbacks (pretentiousness, occasional wrong tone, grumpiness, occasional uncooperativeness) are noted but appear less likely to compromise safety than the organizational and feedback‑receptivity issues of A, the communication deficits of C, or the leadership/arrogance concerns of D.
- **Candidate C** – 100 % reliable, makes quick correct decisions, creates a positive crew atmosphere, and prioritises crew welfare—excellent for reliability and teamwork. However, difficulty communicating ideas, an egocentric attitude, and a lack of willingness to pursue further education raise concerns for crew coordination and long‑term professional development.
- **Candidate D** – Strong concentration, problem‑solving, and a serious sense of responsibility, valuable for handling unexpected events. Yet arrogance, a hot temper, and relatively weak leadership could undermine crew cohesion and command effectiveness.

Balancing reliability, technical competence, judgment under pressure, decision‑making, teamwork/communication, and long‑term suitability, **Candidate B** offers the most well‑rounded profile for a long‑distance pilot given the current evidence. While his interpersonal issues should be monitored, they do not outweigh his strong technical skills, stress tolerance, and conscientiousness.

**Vote:** Candidate B
