## Shared Mental Model (Agent 1)

### Task Summary
**Goal:** Choose the best candidate for a long‑distance pilot position.  
**Candidates:** A, B, C, D  

### Candidate Summary (key points)

| Candidate | Evidence For | Evidence Against |
|-----------|--------------|------------------|
| **A** | Anticipates dangerous situations; sees complex connections; excellent spatial vision; very good leadership qualities | Sometimes not good at taking criticism; can be unorganized; noted as a “show‑off” (agent 2) |
| **B** | Very conscientious; handles stress very well (agent 3); good at assessing weather; excellent computer skills | Can be grumpy; can be uncooperative |
| **C** | Makes correct decisions quickly; **100 % reliable** (re‑stated by me); creates a positive crew atmosphere | Difficulty communicating ideas; regarded as egocentric; not very willing to further his education |
| **D** | Responds adequately to unexpected events; concentrates very well; solves problems extremely well; takes responsibility seriously | Regarded as arrogant; relatively weak leadership skills |

### My Position
- **My Last Vote:** Candidate C  
- **Current Working Favorite:** Candidate C  
- **Rationale:** The explicit claim of 100 % reliability is essential for long‑haul safety and outweighs the communication and learning‑attitude concerns.  
- **Confidence:** 70 %

### Other Agents' Positions
| Agent   | Latest Vote | Evidence Contributed |
|--------|--------------|----------------------|
| **agent_2** | Candidate A | Excellent spatial vision; “show‑off” attitude |
| **agent_3** | Candidate B | Handles stress very well |

### Emerging Group View
- No unanimous decision yet; each candidate has strong assets and notable drawbacks.  
- All agents acknowledge **Candidate C’s 100 % reliability** as a decisive advantage.

### Important Agreements
- Reliability is non‑negotiable; Candidate C is the only one with an explicit 100 % reliability claim.  
- Ability to handle unexpected events (A, D) and stress (B) are valued but not decisive alone.

### Important Disagreements / Tensions
- **Leadership & criticism:** A’s strong leadership is offset by poor response to criticism; D’s leadership is weak.  
- **Cooperation & attitude:** B’s grumpiness/uncooperativeness and A’s “show‑off” demeanor may harm crew cohesion.  
- **Communication & learning:** C’s difficulty communicating and reluctance to pursue further education could affect coordination on extended flights.

### Open Questions
1. How will Candidate C’s communication deficits impact crew coordination during long‑haul operations?  
2. Can A’s “show‑off” behavior be mitigated through supervision or training?  
3. Would targeted leadership development make D a viable option despite current weakness?  
4. Is there a practical way to address B’s uncooperative tendencies without losing his stress‑resilience?

### What Would Change the Decision
- Evidence that another candidate matches or exceeds C’s reliability **and** demonstrates superior communication and leadership.  
- Viable mitigation plans for the identified drawbacks of A, B, or D that the committee finds acceptable.

### Next‑Step Focus
- Evaluate the operational impact of C’s communication issues.  
- Discuss possible training or mentorship to curb A’s “show‑off” attitude and improve B’s cooperation.  
- Consider whether D’s strong problem‑solving can compensate for his leadership gap with appropriate support.
